## The Are.na Problem

Are.na is beautiful. The interface is minimal, the community is thoughtful, and the "slow web" philosophy resonates with designers tired of attention-grabbing algorithms.

But here's the catch: Are.na's pricing jumped significantly in recent years. For many designers — especially freelancers and students — the Pro tier is hard to justify.

**Are.na Pro pricing:**
- $96/year for individuals
- Limited to 5,000 items on Pro
- Must pay for additional storage

If you're looking for a free alternative to Are.na that doesn't sacrifice the experience, this guide is for you.

---

## What Makes Are.na Special (And What to Replace)

Are.na succeeded because of:

1. **Curated discovery** - Not algorithmic, but human-curated
2. **Minimal interface** - Distraction-free browsing
3. **Connections** - Linking items creates new relationships
4. **Slow web philosophy** - No likes, no comments, just content

**What breaks the deal:**
1. Price point for serious users
2. Storage limits on Pro
3. Limited export options
4. No desktop app

## Free Alternatives Compared

### Mare

Mare was built specifically for designers who need to manage visual references:

**What it does well:**
- Visual-first search — find by uploading a reference image
- Tag system — organize however you think
- Browser extension — capture from anywhere
- No item limits on free tier
- Export anytime

**Limitations:**
- Still growing community
- Fewer pre-made collections

**Best for:** Designers who prioritize findability over discovery

---

### Pinterest

Free, but not really a design tool:

**What it does well:**
- Everything is free
- Massive database
- Mobile apps

**What breaks the deal:**
- Algorithm controls what you see
- Ads everywhere
- Hard to find your own saves
- Not built for professional work

**Best for:** Consumer inspiration, not design reference

---

### Raindrop.io

Bookmark manager with visual features:

**What it does well:**
- Free tier includes 1,000 bookmarks
- Browser extensions
- Works with articles AND images
- Collaboration features

**Limitations:**
- Not visual-first
- UI is cluttered
- Searching isn't as strong

**Best for:** General bookmark management with some visual support

---

### Evernote/Web Clipper

Not designed for visual:

**What it does well:**
- Capture anything
- Text search is strong
- Cross-platform

**What breaks the deal:**
- Not designed for images
- Cluttered interface
- Expensive for premium

**Best for:** Text-heavy reference, not visual work

---

### The Matrix Method (Not an App)

Some designers use:

- **Unsaved folder in Finder** + **Apple Photos**
- **Notion** for organization + **Dropbox** for images
- **Obsidian** with image handling

**Pros:** Free, you control everything
**Cons:** No search across images, fragmented, manual

**Best for:** Designers who love tinkering with their system

## How to Migrate from Are.na (If You're Leaving)

If you're switching from Are.na to a free alternative:

### Step 1: Export Your Data

Are.na allows JSON export of your content. Go to Settings → Export.

### Step 2: Choose Your Destination

For Mare specifically:
1. Create free account
2. Use browser extension to upload in batches
3. Recreate your channels as tags
4. Use the visual search to find similar items

### Step 3: Rebuild Your System

Are.na's channel system doesn't translate perfectly. Think about:

- **How do I actually search for things?** — Build your tag system around that
- **What collections matter?** — Only keep what you reference
- **What can I delete?** — Most designers find 60%+ of their Are.na content was saved but never viewed
