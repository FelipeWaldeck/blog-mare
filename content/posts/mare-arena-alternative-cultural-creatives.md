Are.na changed how we collect and share visual culture.

It's where you discover what others are saving. Where you build collaborative channels with people who get your references. Where Tarkovsky stills sit next to Bourdieu quotes and Björk album art.

But here's the gap nobody talks about.

## The References You Can't Share Yet

Your most important references don't fit on Are.na.

That Superstudio collage you saved at 2 AM. The Tiqqun paragraph about chaos that connects to a film you saw last month. The Windows XP interface that feels like the answer to a question you haven't asked.

These references are too early. Too raw. Too personal to contextualize for others.

So they end up scattered. Screenshots in Downloads. Instagram saves you'll never find again. Notes apps with broken image links. Bookmarks to pages that no longer exist.

## The Problem Isn't Organization. It's Timing.

Are.na works when you know what you're looking at — when you can tag it, connect it to other ideas, share it with people who will understand.

But cultural research doesn't happen in public. It happens in the space between seeing something and understanding why it matters. That space can last hours. Months. Years.

**You need a place for references before they're ready for Are.na.**

## Mare: Built for the In-Between Space

Mare is the Are.na alternative for private visual research.

Instead of forcing you to categorize and explain, Mare lets you collect without commitment. Drop images, text, links, film stills, album artwork, theory excerpts. Don't tag them yet. Don't title them. Don't connect them to anything.

Just get them out of your head and into a place you can find again.

When you're ready — when the connection becomes clear, when the project emerges, when you finally understand why you saved that thing — you can organize. Not before.

## Your Visual Universe Has Two Phases

**Phase 1: Private Accumulation**  
Collecting without knowing why. Trusting your gut. Building a personal canon that only makes sense to you. Film stills next to architecture photos next to philosophy quotes. No rhyme. No reason. Yet.

**Phase 2: Public Curation**  
Selecting, contextualizing, sharing. Turning private references into public statements. This is where Are.na shines.

Are.na is perfect for Phase 2.  
Mare is built for Phase 1.

## Who This Is For

- Art directors building personal visual libraries
- Cultural researchers connecting art, film, music, theory
- Artists curating reference collections that span decades
- Curators (professional or personal) organizing visual research
- Anyone who's ever thought: *"I know I saved that reference somewhere..."

## Some References Belong to Your Private Universe

The Superstudio collage at 2 AM. The Tiqqun quote you don't fully grasp yet. The album artwork that feels like an answer.

Keep them. Find them. Let them connect to each other in ways you can't explain yet.

When you're ready to share, you'll know.
